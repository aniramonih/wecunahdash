Early Life

Ryan Lee Stiles was born on April 22, 1959, in Seattle, Washington, to his parents Sonny Stiles and Irene Stiles. He grew up in Vancouver, Canada.

He went to R.C. Palmer Junior and later attended Richmond Senior Secondary. Stiles did not complete his graduation as he was a shy kid.

In 1988, Ryan Stiles got married to Patricia McDonald after dating each other for a long time. The couple has three children named Claire Stiles, Mackenzie Stiles, and Sam Stiles. He resides in Lake Samish, Washington. He holds both American and Canadian citizenships.

Stiles is the owner of a small theatre called Upfront Theatre. He has raised funds for children who suffer from burn injuries. He has raised more than $500,000 for Burned Children Recovery Center.

Career

Soon after leaving his school, Ryan Stiles started performing stand-up comedy in Vancouver. He later became the head writer of CTV’s ‘The Don Harron Show’. He has also assisted in starting the Punchlines Comedy Club. Following that, he became the host of CBC’s ‘Comedy College’.

In 1990, Stiles was seen in ‘Whose Line Is It Anyway?’. He appeared in it till 2006 and again from 2013 till present. In 1995, he became the main cast of ‘The Drew Carey Show’. He has also guest appeared in ‘Dharma & Greg’ and ‘Reno 911!’.

He had a recurring role in ‘Two and a Half Men’ in 2004. In 2011, he started appearing as a recurring performer in ‘Drew Carey’s Improv-A-Ganza’.

He made his film debut by starring in the 1985 movie ‘Rainbow War’. In 1991, he was seen in the film ‘Hot Shots!’. Following that, he went on to appear in ‘Hot Shots! Part Deux’ and ‘Mad About You’. In 2006, he was seen in the film ‘The Extra’.

Stiles has also lent his voice to the movies ‘Astro Boy’, ‘Spooky Buddies’, and ‘Treasure Buddies’. In 2005, he was seen in the mockumentary called ‘Conker: Celebrity Squirrel’.
