Who is Vera Farmiga?

Vera Farmiga is an American actress best known for her roles in the Conjuring Universe films The Conjuring, The Conjuring 2, Annabelle Comes Home, and The Conjuring: The Devil Made Me Do It as paranormal investigator Lorraine Warren.

Vera Farmiga bio

Farmiga was born in Clifton, New Jersey, on August 6, 1973.

Her parents are Lubomyra “Luba” (née Spas), a schoolteacher, and Michael Farmiga, a former systems analyst who is now a gardener.

Vera Farmiga siblings

She has five younger siblings, namely Taissa, Stephan, Nadia, Alexander, and Laryssa (who was born with spina bifida). She also has an older brother named Victor.

Vera Farmiga education

She earned her diploma from Hunterdon Central Regional High School in 1991. She discovered acting there during her junior year after being benched during a varsity soccer match; a friend persuaded her to attend an audition for the school’s production of The Vampire, and she was cast as Lady Margaret.

Vera Farmiga career

Vera starred on Broadway in “Taking Sides” and performed the roles of Miranda in “The Tempest” at the American Conservatory Theater and Anne Hartman in “Good” at The Barrow Group in 1996.

She made her television debut in 1997, co-starring with Heath Ledger in the Fox series “Roar” and played Emily Elliot in the TV movie “Rose Hill.”

In addition to her performances in “The Opportunists” (2000), “Autumn in New York” (2000), “15 Minutes” (2001), “Love in the Time of Money” (2002), and “Dummy,” Farmiga made her big-screen debut in 1998’s “Return to Paradise” (2002). She acted in the 2001 television film “Snow White: The Fairest of Them All” and played Alex Cross on NBC’s “UC: Undercover” from 2001 to 2002.

In 2004, Vera featured alongside Denzel Washington and Meryl Streep in “The Manchurian Candidate,” as suffragist Ruza Wenclawska in the TV movie “Iron Jawed Angels,” and played Detective Susan Branca on the USA Network series “Touching Evil.”

Following that, Farmiga starred in the movies “Orphan” (2008), “Running Scared” (2008), “Breaking and Entering” (2008), “Joshua” (2007), and “Nothing But the Truth” (2008). (2009).

She co-starred with Leonardo DiCaprio, Matt Damon, and Jack Nicholson in Martin Scorsese’s “The Departed” in 2006 as Dr. Madolyn Madden, and she played Elsa Hoess in “The Boy in the Striped Pyjamas” in 2008.

For her portrayal as Alex Goran in the 2009 film “Up in the Air,” Vera received an Academy Award nomination. The following year, the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences inducted her into their hall of fame.

Farmiga portrayed Norma Louise Bates on “Bates Motel” from 2013 to 2017, a role that lasted for 50 episodes over five seasons and resulted in a Primetime Emmy nomination. She costarred with Patrick Wilson as demonologist Ed Warren in “The Conjuring” in 2013 as real-life clairvoyant Lorraine Warren.

The movie “The Conjuring” ($319.5 million) became a huge success and launched the “Conjuring Universe” franchise, which as of this writing has eight movies.

In addition to “The Conjuring,” Vera has appeared in three of these movies: “The Conjuring 2” from 2016, “Annabelle Comes Home” from 2019, and “The Conjuring: The Devil Made Me Do It” from 2021.

These movies each brought in $200 million or more. Ricky Gervais wrote and directed the comedy “Special Correspondents” in 2016, and Farmiga later made a guest appearance on “Philip K. Dick’s Electric Dreams” in 2018.

She starred as Dr. Emma Russell in the 2019 movie “Godzilla: King of the Monsters,” which earned $386.6 million at the box office. She also acted in the movies “The Commuter,” “Boundaries,” “The Front Runner,” “Skin,” and “Captive State.”

She played Elizabeth Lederer in the critically acclaimed “When They See Us” Netflix miniseries in 2019.

Vera appeared as Livia Soprano in the “The Many Saints of Newark” prequel to “The Sopranos” in 2021. She also made a special appearance on the Netflix series “Halston” and portrayed Eleanor Bishop in the Disney+ Marvel series “Hawkeye.”
