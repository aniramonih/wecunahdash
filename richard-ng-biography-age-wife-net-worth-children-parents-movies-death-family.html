Biography

Richard Ng Yiu-hon (born December 27, 1939), also known as Richard Woo, was a prominent actor from Hong Kong. He was celebrated for his adept portrayal of comedic roles, particularly in the vibrant Hong Kong film scene of the 1980s and 1990s.

Richard Ng graced the screen in 80 films throughout his career. His performances in Winners and Sinners and Beyond the Sunset earned him nominations for the Best Actor Award at the Hong Kong Film Awards on two occasions. He shared the screen with Hong Kong action cinema stars, such as Jackie Chan, Michelle Yeoh, Andy Lau, and Jet Li.

Early Life & Education

Richard Ng, a celebrated actor, was born on December 27, 1939, in Guangzhou, Guangdong, China. He was raised by his father, Ng Hoi Lam, and spent his formative years in the United Kingdom.

Born into a prosperous Hong Kong family that owned the Kowloon Funeral Parlour and Kowloon Motor Bus brands, as reported by Dimsum Daily, Richard Ng‘s life took a poignant turn at the tender age of 15. Following the tragic loss of his mother, his father made the difficult decision to send him to the United Kingdom for his education.

Richard Ng, a renowned actor celebrated for his comedic roles in Hong Kong cinema, has a rich educational background across various prestigious institutions. He attended Eton College, St. Stephen’s College Preparatory School, and St. Stephen’s College, Hong Kong, which provided him with a strong foundation in education and possibly contributed to his later success in the entertainment industry.

Career

Richard Ng, a familiar face in over 80 films, made quite a mark in Hong Kong cinema. You might remember him from classics like Winners and Sinners and Beyond the Sunset, roles that earned him nominations for the Best Actor Award at the Hong Kong Film Awards.

Let’s rewind a bit to the ’70s, ’80s, and ’90s. Richard kicked off his career alongside Sammo Hung in The Private Eyes and kept the momentum going with the Lucky Stars series. Did you know he even tried directing with Murder Most Foul in 1979?

Richard Ng‘s character, Exhaust Pipe, in Winners and Sinners, became iconic, paving the way for his recurring role as Sandy in later films. And who could forget the hilarious Pom Pom series, in which he and John Shum played a pair of clumsy cops?

Fast-forward to the 2000s. Richard made a big move to London but didn’t slow down. He entertained us, appearing on British TV in shows like River City and playing the emperor of China in a Channel 4 docu-drama.

But here’s the heartwarming part: Richard Ng teamed up with his son Carl Ng for some on-screen magic. They appeared together in films like Love at First Sight and Legend of the Dragon. Talk about a family affair!

Even in more recent years, Richard’s cameo in the 2016 flick Skiptrace reminds us why he’s a legend in the game.

Personal Life

Richard Ng tied the knot with Susan Ng in 1965, a talented British stylist who worked with Vidal Sassoon in London and New York. Their union blessed them with four wonderful children: Karl Ng, Zoe Ng, Louise Ng, and Alex Ng.

Richard‘s educational journey occurred in London, England, where he moved from Hong Kong in 1997. Susan, Richard‘s beloved wife, excelled in her career and had the honor of styling Bruce Lee‘s hair during the 1970s through her business, HAIR BY SUSAN.

Their children followed diverse paths: Alex, now a live event producer overseeing a concert production company called INTERNATIONAL FIXER; Zoe, who pursued her passion for dance while exploring nature around the globe; Louise, who delved into photography; and Carl, who pursued a career in acting.

Sadly, in March 2021, the family faced a challenging time when one of their daughters was arrested for marijuana trafficking, followed by another arrest of their second daughter later that month.

Richard Ng‘s health had been a concern for years. He revealed in 2019 that he required daily dialysis due to kidney issues and underwent surgery for a cardiac embolism in 2021.

Tragically, at the age of 83, Richard passed away. Reports indicate that despite efforts by medical professionals to revive him after his heart stopped, he couldn’t be saved. His family gathered by his side on April 9, praying for him as he was surrounded by tubes. Eventually, the doctors had to confirm his passing.
