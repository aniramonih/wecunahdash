Pete Hegseth Biography

Pete Hegseth is an American well-known author and Television host at FOX News Channel (FNC). Hegseth is also an Army National Guard officer. Before FNC, he was the former executive director of political advocacy groups Vets For Freedom and Concerned Veterans for America.

Pete attended Forest Lake Area High School in Forest Lake, Minnesota. Later on, he attended Princeton University in 2003 graduating with a Bachelor of Arts. Hegseth received a Master of Public Policy from the John F. Kennedy School of Government at Harvard University. Hegseth was also the publisher of The Princeton Tory, a magazine of conservative political thought student-run publication.

In addition, Hegseth serves as a co-host of FOX News Channel’s (FNC) FOX & Friends Weekend from 6 to 10 AM/ ET. He hosted FNC’s special New Year’s coverage and also provided analysis and commentary across the network’s daytime and primetime programming. Pete joined the network as a contributor in 2014. Pete also lost the Republican Party endorsement for Senate election in Minnesota to Kurt Bills in 2012. Later in 2017, he interviewed former President Donald Trump receiving criticism for allowing the latter to make fake statements without challenging him.

Hegseth has also worked at the Manhattan Institute for Policy Research. He is also a contributor to National Review Online and a term member of the Council on Foreign Relations. Nevertheless, as an author, he has written for the Washington Post, The Washington Times, The Wall Street Journal, and New York Post. Pete is also the New York best-selling author of American Crusade, Modern Warriors, and In the Arena.

Pete was commissioned into the U.S. Army National Guard as an infantry officer in 2003. In 2014, he served as a guard at Guantanamo Bay in Cuba. Afterward, he served as an infantry platoon leader and civil-military operations officer in Baghdad and Samarra after returning from Cuba.
