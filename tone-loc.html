Tone Loc Biography

Tone Loc born Anthony Terrell Smith is an American Rapper, Actor and Producer. He was born on 3 March 1966 in Los Angeles, California, United States. He is famously known for his raspy voice used to make his hit songs “Wild Thing” and “Funky Cold Medina”, for which he was nominated for a Grammy Award and for being featured in “We’re All in the Same Gang”, a collaborative single by the West Coast Rap All-Stars.

Tone Loc Career and Music

Loc was educated at the Hollywood Professional School where he performed as a teenager with the Triple A rap group. His debut album, Lōc-ed After Dark, released in January 1989. The video for the first single, “Wild Thing,” became common scene on MTV in the US. The song rose to No. 2 on the US Hot 100, and to the top twenty in Australia. The second single, “Funky Cold Medina,” also was a hit. It reached the top 5 in the US, peaking at No. 3; the top ten in Australia; and the top twenty in the UK. The album Lōc-ed After Dark peaked at No. 1 in the US. Since its release, has been certified 2× platinum.

He released a second album Cool Hand Lōc in November 1991. Its first single was “All Through the Night”, which found success, reaching No. 80 on the Billboard Hot 100 and #16 on the Rap Songs chart. It was frequently played on MTV and BET.His song “Ace Is in the House” is featured in the films Ace Ventura: Pet Detective (1994) and Ace Ventura Jr: Pet Detective (2009). He appeared as Albert in Touched by an Angel, season 2 episode 8, which aired November 11, 1995.

Tone Lōc has played in several feature films, including Poetic Justice, Blank Check and Posse. He has also been a voice actor, voicing characters in several television cartoon series such as King of the Hill and C-Bear and Jamal and was featured in the animated film Bébé’s Kids, playing the wise-cracking baby Pee Wee. He is the voice of the character Lou the Goanna in the 1992 film FernGully: The Last Rainforest. He has voiced for episodes of Uncle Grandpa, and Chowder, both airing on Cartoon Network.
