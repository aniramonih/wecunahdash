Daneliya Tuleshova Bio

Daneliya Tuleshova is a Kazakh child singer who represented Kazakhstan at the 2018 Junior Eurovision Song Contest in Minsk, Belarus, finishing sixth with her song “Ózińe sen”

She won the fourth season of The Voice Kids Ukraine before Junior Eurovision and was a finalist in Children’s New Wave 2015. She has participated in The World’s Best in 2019, representing her country alongside Dimash Kudaibergen.

Tuleshova took part in the international singing competition, Hopes of Europe, in January 2017 where she received the grand prize. She auditioned for The Voice Kids Ukraine season 4 later the same year. She sang Demi Lovato’s song “Stone Cold” during the Blind Auditions.

Monatik, Natalia Mohylevska and Vremya I Steklo, all three coaches, “turned their tables” for her; she preferred Monatik. She has made it to the grand final after passing all the stages.

There, she performed the song “Ne tvoya viyna” and was ultimately declared as the competition’s winner. She was at the time the first non-Ukrainian to compete but Georgian singer Oleksandr Zazarashvyli won the fifth season in 2019.

Daneliya Tuleshova Eurovision 2018

She came to limelight in 2017 after she won the fourth season of The Voice Kids Ukraine. She had won the 2015 Junior New Wave singing competition, before appearing on The Voice Kids. Her cover of “Ne tvoya viina” a song by Okean Elzy earned more than 2 million views on YouTube in less than a month after it was posted. Daneliya was selected to represent Kazakhstan at the 2018 Junior Eurovision Song Contest in Minsk, Belarus after winning the Kazakh national final selection.

Daneliya Tuleshova Awards

She was noticed at the age of eight by the organizers of the Kazakh singing competition Ayaglagan Astana, the selection round for the popular Children’s New Wave, an international contest held annually in Russia for young performers. Daneliya entered the competition in 2015 and reached Children’s New Wave final, where she received the Audience Award.

In 2019 Tuleshova auditioned to perform the song Rise Up, originally performed by Andra Day, for the American TV talent show The World’s Best. She defeated Japan’s Manami Ito in the fight rounds to qualify for the Champion rounds. She performed “What About Us” there, but was eventually eliminated from the competition, putting her in the top 8.
