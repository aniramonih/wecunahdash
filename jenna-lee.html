‘Fox News’ anchor Jenna Lee Career

Straight out of UC Santa Barbara, Lee embarked on her journalism career as a freelance writer for the Marina Times. This was a local newspaper based in San Francisco. The soon to be anchor stayed with the paper for two years, before she found herself at Columbia University.

While here, she would work for NY1 while moonlighting for Forbes, a celebrity gossip site. The latter proved the more lucrative venture, and she subsequently left her work at NY1 to pursue it full time.

Newsreader, Jenna Lee

Her big break finally came in 2007 when she was personally brought on by the CEO of Fox News to co-anchor the Fox Business News.  Lee’s charm shone through on the show, prompting the network to add her as an additional correspondent to the Money for the Breakfast show. Once that show was done, she was moved to a different show, “Imus in the Morning.”

Jenna officially joined the Fox News Channel in 2010, when she was brought on to co-host the Happening Now, with Jon Scott.  She would stay on the show for a good eight years, before bowing out to pursue other interests.

Jenna Lee Married to husband, Leif Babin.

The stunning broadcaster is, in fact, a married woman. And if that isn’t enough to put off anyone who might have had a crush on her, then maybe the fact that her husband is a Navy Seal will. Jenna is married to Leif Babin, an American soldier. The pair first met in 2008 at a fundraiser for the troops.

Sparks must have flown in between them that night as they were married three years later. The wedding took place on the 3rd of July, 2011 in Wimberley, Texas. Jenna’s husband is a decorated war hero who has served his country proudly. Some of his achievements include: being awarded a Purple Heart, a Silver Star and a Bronze Star for his efforts in Anbar Province, Iraq. Leif and his wife Jenna have two children together: Liberty Josephine and Babin Brian Marc Babin.
