Who is Kevin O Leary?

Kevin O'Leary, a Canadian entrepreneur and television personality, is best known for his role as a "shark" on the hit reality show "Shark Tank." Born on July 9, 1954, in Montreal, Quebec, O'Leary has established himself as a prominent figure in the world of business and finance.

O'Leary's early career began with a focus on television production and later shifted towards the software industry, where he co-founded Softkey Software Products. The company's success led to its acquisition by Mattel in 1999 for a staggering $3.7 billion, making O'Leary a millionaire. After this lucrative exit, he ventured into various investment opportunities and became known for his financial expertise.

His rise to widespread fame came with his participation in "Shark Tank," where he joined as one of the original "sharks" in 2009. On the show, he evaluates business pitches and potential investments, known for his blunt and no-nonsense approach. His catchphrases like "Mr. Wonderful" and "You're dead to me" have become iconic in the show's context.

O'Leary is an accomplished author, public speaker, and investor. He has authored books like "Cold Hard Truth" and "Men, Women & Money," sharing his insights on entrepreneurship, finance, and investment. His speaking engagements cover a wide range of topics related to business and economics.
