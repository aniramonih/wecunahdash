Alex Winter Stars Alongside Keanu Reeves In Comedy/Sci-Fi Production 'Bill &amp; Ted Music'

Publish date: 2024-08-23

If you are a fan of Keanu Reeves, then you might not forget his role in the comedy/sci-fi production, Bill & Ted Music. Keanu Reeves starred alongside the American actor and film director, Alex Winter on the project, which went successful throughout the overs.

Alex Winter, who starred as Bill S. Preston in Bill & Ted recently revealed that the first day of filming on 'Bill & Ted Face the Music' was "weirdly nostalgic." After the 55-year-old actor reprised his roles as a time-travelling musician for the first time in 29 years alongside Reeves, he mentioned that it brought back his memories with his co-actor getting back into the iconic phone booth. Likewise, the auditioning video of both Alex Winter and Keanu Reeves went viral in recent days.

Talking to Collider on 21 August, Winter mentioned, 'Oh, we're getting back in the booth again.'

Alex Winter alongside Keanu Reeves in the upcoming 'Bill & Ted' (©: npr.org)

"It was trippy. We had done a lot of rehearsing and Keanu and I had been working on developing the story for years so it wasn't too difficult. But with every movie, there comes a point where you have to cast the rehearsal aside and just do it," he added.

Similarly, Keanu Reeves, well-known as John Wick, the Baba-Yaga said, "Our first day was a really good way to start, we were getting in and out of our phone booth, which is our time-travelling machine."

As per both actors, the highly anticipated movie has finally brought them together. 'It got made because everything came together, it took a long time,' he said. Bill & Ted will release on 27 August 2020 through Orion Pictures. Tight your gears to watch one of the comedy movies of American film history.

How Much Is Alex Winter's Net Worth?

Alex Winter, age 55, has followed a multi-dimensional career as an actor and film director. Similarly, he is a producer and a screenwriter. Through his career, he has accumulated the net worth of $8 million. As an actor, he first starred in the 1985 movie named 'Death Wish 3.' Similarly, he landed his roles in a couple of movies Squeal of Death and The Equalizer in the same year.

Since then, he starred in several movies and tv shows including Medium Rare (1987), Rosalie Goes Shopping (1989), Bill & Ted's Excellent Adventures (1990), The Borrowers (1997), Grand Piano (2013), Into the Dark (2018), Robot Chicken (2012-19), and more.

As a film director, Winter directed the production of Squeal of Death, The Idiot Box, Jimmy Kimmel Live!, Ben 10: Alien Swarm, Downloaded, Bella and the Bulldogs, Trump's Lobby, The Panama Papers, and others.
