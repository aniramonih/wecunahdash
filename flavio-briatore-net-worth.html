Return to Formula One and Race Fixing Scandal

After purchasing the Benetton Formula team in 2000, Flavio Briatore made a triumphant return to the world of Formula One. Renaming the team Renault F1, Briatore continued to lead the organization and achieved notable success during this period. One of his key accomplishments was signing Spanish driver Fernando Alonso, who went on to win the Drivers’ and Constructors’ Championships in 2005 and 2006.

However, Briatore’s return to Formula One was marred by a race fixing scandal that rocked the sport. In 2008, it was discovered that Nelson Piquet Jr., a Renault driver at the time, deliberately crashed during the Singapore Grand Prix to help Alonso secure a victory. This unethical behavior raised serious concerns and led to a major investigation.

As a result of the scandal, Flavio Briatore resigned from his position at Renault and was subsequently banned from participating in any FIA-sanctioned events. However, in 2010, the ban was overturned by the French courts, allowing Briatore to once again be involved in the world of motorsport.
