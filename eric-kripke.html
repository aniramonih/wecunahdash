Career

Eric gained prominence as the creator of The CW fantasy drama series Supernatural (2005-2020), serving as showrunner for the first five seasons. He worked on the 2011 romantic action thriller The Adjustment Bureau as an associate producer. Similarly, he produced the science fiction series Timeless as well as the post-apocalyptic drama Revolution. He developed and is the showrunner of Amazon Prime Video’s superhero series The Boys.

After the popularity of Supernatural, Eric co-wrote the screenplay for the early 2005 picture Boogeyman. Amped, a comic book series by Kripke, was released in the autumn of 2015 after being announced in April of that year. In August 2015, it was revealed that Eric and his writing partner Shawn Ryan were developing the action-adventure series Timeless for NBC. On April 6, 2016, it was revealed that Cinemax was producing a television series based on the comic book, which was being created by Kripke, Evan Goldberg, and Seth Rogen. The series was produced in part by Pont Grey Television, Original Film, and Sony Pictures.
