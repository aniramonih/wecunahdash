Early Life & Biography

Lonzo Ball was born on October 27, 1997, in California, the United States, to Tina and LaVar Ball. Both his parents were former professional basketball players. He started playing the sport at the age of two. While growing up, Lonzo and his brothers idolized LeBron James.

Career

Lonzo started his career in basketball while he was in high school. He was rated as a consensus five-star recruit by major scouting services. As a freshman in college, he was included in the list of fifty players who were being awarded the John R. Wooden Award.

In his first collegiate game, Lonzo scored 19 points and eight rebounds. He led UCLA to a tournament win over Texas A&M for which he was named the MVP of the Wooden Legacy. For the season, he was the only player in the United States to average at least 14 points, six rebounds, and six assists.

He was a unanimous first-team All-American, earning honors from the Associated Press, NABC, and The Sporting News. He was awarded the Wayman Tisdale Award by the USBWA and also received honorable mention for the Pac-12 All-Defensive Team.

In the 2017 NBA draft, he was selected as the second overall pick by the Los Angeles Lakers. In the same season, he averaged 16.3 points, 9.3 assists, 7.7 rebounds, and was named the league MVP.
