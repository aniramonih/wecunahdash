Jamila Abdi Charged With First-Degree Murder for Killing Fariya Yusuf

Publish date: 2024-08-31

Seattle, Washington, March 4, 2023 In conjunction with a fatal shooting, the King County Prosecuting Attorney’s Office accused a woman of murder.

Jamila Abdi, 26, is accused of shooting and killing her companion and has been charged with first-degree murder.

Abdi reportedly took the woman to her apartment, where they parked and remained inside for at least ten minutes, according to court records. The victim then exited the vehicle and headed inside her apartment, leaving Abdi behind.

Following her arrest, Abdi spoke with Seattle police and admitted that she had been discussing the victim’s “toxic” relationship with her boyfriend when her companion went into her apartment to get some wine so they could continue their conversation.

In accordance with court records, texts on the victim’s phone reveal that Abdi attempted to contact her via text and phone call over the course of the subsequent 20 minutes in an effort to persuade her to return outside. Surveillance footage captures Abdi exiting her vehicle and entering the victim’s residence while carrying a gun.

In the end, the victim emerged from the house holding a goblet of wine and her cellphone to her ear. The victim walked toward Abdi while talking to a companion who was listening, according to court records.

According to the charging papers, Abdi pointed the gun at the victim when they were only a few feet apart and fired 14 rounds at him. Then, Abdi was seen leaving in a vehicle traveling north on 42nd Avenue South.

The deceased was originally from Somalia.

On March 10, the Seattle cops detained Abdi. Her bail has been imposed by a judge at $2 million. The arraignment for her is set for March 23.
