Daniela Ruah

Publish date: 2024-08-08

Daniela Ruah is a Portuguese-American actress. She is widely known for her appearance as the  NCIS special agent named Kensi Blye in NCIS: Los Angeles.

Daniela was born as Daniela Sofia Korn Ruah on 2nd December 1983, in Boston, Massachusetts, United States of America. Born to her Portuguese parents Moises Carlos Bentes Ruah and Catarina Lia Katia Azanccot Korn, she is of White ethnicity and holds American nationality. 

Daniela Ruah's marital life

Daniela Ruah is a married woman to David Paul Olsen. The couple got engaged soon after meeting. In December 2013, Daniela and her partner welcomed their first baby, a son named River Isaac Ruah Olsen. 

Later on 17th June 2014, Daniela Ruah and David Olsen exchanged their wedding vows in Portugal. In addition to their first child, the pair welcomed their second baby Sierra Esther Ruah Olsen, in September 2016.

Since the marriage, Ruah and her spouse are living together happily, and there are no rumors or controversies about the couple. 

Daniela began her career from soap operas

The famous actress started her career appearing in soap operas since her early days. At the age of sixteen years, Daniela made her first appearance as Sara on the soap opera Jardins Proibidos.

At the age of eighteen, Daniela shipped to London to study. After her graduation, she went to Portugal to start her acting career. During the time, Ruah also won the celebrity dance contest named Danca Comigo.

Daniela became famous after appearing in NCIS Los Angeles

In 2007, the renowned actress again moved to New York and attended the Lee Strasberg Theatre and Film Institute. On 22nd October 2009, Ruah portrayed as the Special Agent in NCIS: Los Angeles, where Catherine Bell also represented as Sarah.

Daniela gained immense fame from her appearance in the NCIS. She also made her guest appearance on the television named Hawaii Five-0. Ruah also made her appearance in the dramatic play Proof at Los Angeles' Hayworth Theatre in 2013.

The famous actress' recent work includes her appearance as the co-host for Eurovision Song Contest 2018.
