Rep. Lauren Boebert dons a red 'Let's Go Brandon' version of AOC's Met Gala dress at Mar-a-Lago

Publish date: 2024-08-11

Rep. Lauren Boebert showed off her new fashion ‘movement’ after posing for pictures with former President Donald Trump and others at Mar-a-Lago Wednesday in a red dress that said ‘Let’s Go Brandon,’ after the Democrats’ catastrophic election loss in Virginia earlier this week.

At what appeared to be a party celebrating the massive GOP victory, the Colorado Republican donned a showy dress that showcased the now-notoriously anti-Biden phrase. The style of the dress and where the phrase was placed on it mocked Rep. Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez’s now-infamous ‘Tax the Rich’ dress that the New York Democrat and Squad member wore to the Met Gala in September.

Boebert, 34 – who captioned her dress photo with Trump writing, ‘It’s not a phrase, it’s a movement’ – could be seen smiling into the camera while Trump offered a thumbs up. She posed with others, as well. 

‘Winning,’ Matt Schlapp wrote on his Twitter with a photo of him pointing at Boebert’s dress.

Boebert, 34, captioned her dress photo with Trump, ‘It’s not a phrase, it’s a movement’

She also posed with Diamond and Silk, who call themselves Donald Trump’s ‘most loyal supporters’

Rep. Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez wore a ‘Tax the Rich’ dress to the Met Gala in September

Schlapp is the Chairman of the American Conservative Union, the nation’s original grassroots conservative organization and home of CPAC and was former President George W. Bush’s deputy assistant and political director during Bush’s first term.

‘Congresswoman @laurenboebert says everything better than @AOC,’ captioned Trump insider Jessie Jane Duff, who is on the Organizing Committee for Concerned Veterans for America and served 20 years on active duty in the US Marine Corps.

Duff could also be seen in a photo with Boebert on the latter’s Twitter page.

Another picture featuring Boebert and the red ‘Let’s Go Brandon’ dress alongside Diamond and Silk, who call themselves Donald Trump’s ‘most loyal supporters.’  

Boebert has remained a vocal critic of her Democratic colleagues, AOC especially, and their progressive efforts to push Biden’s agenda, telling Fox News last month that ‘the American people aren’t buying Biden’s fast-track to socialism.’   

Republican Glenn Youngkin pulled off a stunning upset to beat Democrat Terry McAuliffe for the Virginia gubernatorial race this week

Republican Glenn Youngkin pulled off a stunning upset to beat Democrat Terry McAuliffe for the Virginia gubernatorial race this week, with a 2.1 lead for the Republican with 99 percent of the vote counted. Youngkin, 54, is the state’s first red Governor since 2009. 

Meanwhile, the story behind the implicitly anti-Biden ‘Let’s Go Brandon’ meme began earlier this month, when NASCAR fans screamed ‘F**k Joe Biden!’ in unison as the winner of Alabama’s Talladega Superspeedway race gave an interview about his victory.

A profane chant mocking Joe Biden broke out at at Alabama’s Talladega Superspeedway after Brandon Brown received his first NASCAR Xfinity Series race victory.

Shortly after Branson Brown gave his post-Xfinity Series race interview, telling a reporter his victory was a ‘dream come true,’ crowds in the stands began to loudly shout in unison, ‘F**k Joe Biden.’

NASCAR originally posted a video of the interview on Twitter, including the chant, but later deleted the post.

Last month, similar chants mocking Joe Biden broke out at college football games for a second straight week in stadiums across Southern states.

They came as the president’s approval ratings dipped following the chaotic evacuation from Afghanistan and his controversial vaccine mandate for federal workers.

The red dress Boebert wore was in specific reference to Ocasio Cortez, 31, who attended the $35,000-a-head Met Gala while wearing a ‘Tax the Rich’ dress earlier this year.

‘And we said, we can’t just play along, but we need to break the fourth wall and challenge some of the institutions, and while the Met is known for its spectacle, we should have a conversation about it,’ Ocasio-Cortez said.
