Career

Stephenie Meyer had a dream about the Twilight novel, and that was its start. She first just wanted to write it, however, in around 90 days she finished the novel. Then, she signed a contract with the publishing company, Little, Brown, and Company for three books.

Her first novel, Twilight, was published in 2005, ranked on The New York Times Best Seller list, was the best book of the year, and was sold across the whole world. After that success, Stephenie Meyer decided to go on with the story in 3 books. The first one was published in 2006, titled New Moon, and it did again rank on The New York Times Best Seller list, the second one was published in 2007, titled Eclipse, and the 2008 Breaking Dawn. Breaking Dawn sold more than 1 million copies in its first 24 hours, and she eventually started earning awards.

Stephenie Meyer’s Twilight novel series was adapted to a romantic fantasy movie series, and it has been one of the most successful movies of all time. The movie series are Twilight, The Twilight Saga: New Moon, The Twilight Saga: Eclipse, The Twilight Saga: Breaking Dawn – Part 1, and The Twilight Saga: Breaking Dawn – Part 2.

Stephenie Meyer has authored several other successful novels, including The Host, and The Chemist. The Host novel was also adapted into a 2013 romantic science-fiction thriller movie titled The Host, and Stephenie was the producer of the movie. She also was the producer of a romantic comedy movie titled Austenland.

Stephenie Meyer is the producer of a 2017-2018 fantasy drama movie titled Down a Dark Hall.
