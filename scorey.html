Scorey Age, Biography, Net Worth, Girlfriend, Height, Real Name

Publish date: 2024-08-20

Bakari Ward, widely recognized by his stage names Scorey or Scorey Ayee, is an accomplished American rapper from Syracuse, New York.

Born on November 26, 1998, the 24-year-old made significant strides in his career when he became the inaugural artist signed to Only Dreamers Achieve Records in 2020.

This record label was established by renowned Chicago rapper Polo G. He holds Polo G in high regard, considering him a mentor and guiding influence in his musical journey.

With his association with Polo G, he has often been referred to as Polo G’s protégé.

Profile Summary

Early Life & Background

Bakari Ward’s musical journey was deeply influenced by the iconic sounds of rappers like Lil Wayne, G Herbo, and 50 Cent, whose music resonated with him during his formative years.

Inspired by their styles, Ward found himself mimicking their flows and engaging in freestyle sessions with his friends. It was during these impromptu rap sessions that his passion for music began to take shape.

In 2018, Ward made a pivotal decision to step into the recording booth and bring his artistic vision to life.

However, it wasn’t until the following year, in 2019, that he truly dedicated himself to his craft, taking his musical pursuits seriously and setting his sights on achieving greatness.

While music became an essential part of Ward’s life, basketball held a special place in his heart during his childhood.

Tragedy struck Ward’s life as he lost close friends along the way. One of these friends, Leah, became a recurring reference in his songs, a way for him to pay homage and keep their memory alive.

Another cherished friend, Zachary “Zuly” Holloway, was featured prominently in his early music videos, their bond serving as a source of inspiration for Ward’s creative expression.

Ward’s poignant song “Victim” was even filmed during a gathering held in remembrance of Holloway’s untimely passing, demonstrating the profound influence of his friendships on his art.

Near Death Experience

At the age of 21, Ward faced a severe incident that nearly cost him his life. After unknowingly ingesting counterfeit Percocet pills laced with the deadly drug fentanyl, he found himself on the brink of death.

Thankfully, Ward received immediate medical attention when he blacked out, and the experience served as a wake-up call. From that moment on, he made a conscious decision to abstain from Percocet, recognizing the dangers of substance abuse and prioritizing his well-being.

Life’s challenges sometimes lead individuals down unexpected paths, and for Ward, it meant working as a bathroom cleaner at Green Lake State Park. Unfortunately, this job inadvertently exposed him to a dark cycle of drug abuse.

Professional Career

Ward’s ascent in the music industry can be traced back to a pivotal moment when his debut single, “Freddie Krueger,” made waves after its release on September 1, 2019. It was through the power of social media that his talent caught the attention of none other than rapper Polo G.

A fortunate chain of events led Polo G to stumble upon the track after seeing it posted on a friend’s Instagram story, who happened to be signed to the same record label as Scorey.

Intrigued by what he heard, Polo G reached out to the young artist directly through Instagram, marking the beginning of an extraordinary journey for the budding rapper.

Just days after their initial contact, he received an unexpected invitation from Polo G himself to perform at Webster Hall in the heart of New York City.

This unforgettable opportunity arose as part of Polo G’s Die a Legend tour, where Scorey shared the stage with acclaimed artists like Yungeen Ace, Luh Kel, and Toosii.

With his star on the rise and Polo G recognizing his immense potential, Scorey found himself at a crossroads. It was during the recording session for his single “Moods” that Polo G extended an extraordinary offer.

He invited Scorey to join Only Dreamers Achieve, a groundbreaking record label that Polo G had established in partnership with Columbia Records.

Recognizing the immense opportunity before him, he graciously accepted, officially becoming the very first artist to sign with the label.

Under the guidance and mentorship of Polo G, Scorey embarked on a new chapter in his musical journey.

The first fruit of this collaboration manifested in the release of his single “Dior You” on March 12, 2021, serving as his inaugural offering under the esteemed Only Dreamers Achieve label.

The story continued to unfold as Scorey’s remarkable talent led him to collaborate with Polo G once again. This time, it was for Polo G’s highly anticipated third studio album, “Hall of Fame,” released in June 2021.

His music has become a source of solace, inspiration, and empowerment for a generation searching for a voice that represents their experiences.
