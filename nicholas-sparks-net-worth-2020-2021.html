Early Life

Nicholas Charles Sparks was born in 1965 in Omaha, Nebraska. He was raised as a roman catholic and had German, Irish, and English ancestry. He started writing as a hobby from his school, where he wrote his first novel ‘The passing’ (Unpublished).

Sparks completed his graduation in Business finance honors at the University of Notre Dame, where he also met his wife to be Cathy Cote. After their marriage in 1989, the couple moved to North Carolina.

In 1990, Sparks co-wrote a book with Billy Mills, which was published by Feather Publications. After its Success, Sparks dedicated his time to writing his breakthrough novel, ‘The Notebook.’ Upon its release in 1996, the novel made it to the New York Best Seller list in its first-week release.

Although the career graph of Sparks never went down with his books turning into movie adaptations, his marriage life saw its end. Sparks and Cathy got divorced in 2015.

Career

Nicholas Sparks’ career began when he teamed up with Billy Wills for a book called Woking: A Lakota Journey to Happiness and Self-understanding. His first breakthrough was his book, The Notebook, which became very popular and earned him his first million-dollar movie deal.

He later moved to North Carolina and started working on his next novels. His other bestselling novel, ‘A Walk to Remember,’ was also adapted as filmmaking him a globally popular writer.

In 2016, his novel, ‘Two by Two,’ sold around 98,000 copies during the first-week release.
