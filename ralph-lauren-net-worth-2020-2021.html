Early Life

Ralph Lauren was born on October 14, 1939, in The Bronx, New York, to his parents Frank Lifshitz and Frieda Lifshitz. He has three siblings.

He went to Marsha Stern Talmudical Academy and completed his high school from DeWitt Clinton High School. Following that, Lauren attended City University of New York’s Baruch College and studied business but dropped out soon.

In 1964, Ralph Lauren got married to Ricky Ann Low-Beer. The couple has three children named Andrew Lauren, David Lauren, and Dylan Lauren. In 1987, he underwent surgery for removing a brain tumor.

Ralph Lauren is known as an automobile collector. His collections include ‘Ferrari Testarossas,’ ‘Porsche 997 GT3 RS’, ‘Bugatti Veyron,’ ‘Mercedes-Benz 300 SL Gullwing’, ‘1930 Mercedes-Benz SSK’, ‘Bugatti Type 57SC Atlantic’, ‘1929 Bentley 4½ Litre’, ‘1962 Ferrari 250 GTO’, and much more.

Career

Ralph Lauren started his career by getting enrolled in the army. He was in the U.S. Army from 1962 to 1964. It was followed by working as a sales assistant for Brooks Brothers. He then worked for Beau Brummel.

In 1967, he became the founder of ‘Ralph Lauren Corporation,’ a company known for making apparel, fragrances, and home accessories. He was also interested in sports and named his first line of menswear as ‘Polo.’ In 1971, the corporation launched tailored women shirts. In the same year, he also opened the Ralph Lauren Store in Beverly Hills, California.

In 1978, he started making Ralph Lauren fragrances. Later he started making a cologne for men and fragrances for women. In 1981, the company became international and ventured into the European market. In 1986, Ralph Lauren began his flagship in Rhinelander mansion. His company became public in 1997 and was traded on the New York Stock Exchange.

In 1999, Lauren started the “RL Restaurant.” He is also the owner of two other restaurants.

In 2008, his corporation created the brand ‘American Living, ’ which was for JC Penney.

Awards & Achievements

In 2007, Ralph Lauren won the American Fashion Legend Award at the CFDA Awards. In 2000, he was inducted into the Fashion Walk of Fame. In 1997, he won the Humanitarian Leadership Award by the CFDA. In 1985, he was conferred with America’s Designer Achievement Award by the Menswear Association. He has also won the Coty “Winnie” Award. In 1975, he was the winner of the American Fashion Award.
