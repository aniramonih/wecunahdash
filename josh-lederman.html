Josh Lederman Nbc | Msnbc

He is a national political reporter for NBC News based in Washington. Josh also frequently appears on MSNBC, NBC News, and all other NBC platforms. He also covered the presidential campaign, Political investigations as well as national security.

Josh joined the NBC in July 2018 working as the National Political Reporter until February 2021 when he became correspondent. Prior to joining NBC News, Josh worked for The Associated Press in Washington, DC from June 2012 to June 2018. Moreover, from June 2012 to January 2017, he worked as a white house reporter, and from January 2017 to June 2018 Josh worked as a foreign policy reporter.

While at The Press, Josh covered President Barack Obama and Vice President Joe Biden. In addition, he covered foreign affairs, national security as well as U.S. diplomacy for The AP. Notably, Josh won the 2015 White House Correspondents’ Association’s Merriman Smith award for excellence in presidential news coverage under deadline pressure. He won the award for his coverage of a security breach that let a fence-jumper make it inside the White House.

He began his career in the Associated Press’s Jerusalem bureau during the Arab Spring. Later, Josh covered Gov. Chris Christie and state politics for the AP in New Jersey. Moreover, he covered presidential, House, and Senate campaigns for The Hill newspaper in Washington as a white hose reporter in 2011-2012.
