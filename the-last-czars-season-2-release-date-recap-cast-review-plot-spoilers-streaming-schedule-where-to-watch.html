The Storyline of The Last Czars

The Last Czars seamlessly weaves together elements of drama and historical events. Centered around the rule of Nicholas II, the final emperor of Russia’s Romanov Dynasty, the series chronicles his ascent to the throne in 1894, culminating in the tragic execution of Nicholas II and his family in 1918. While the narrative is predominantly driven by dramatic elements, it incorporates approximately 80% fictional drama and 20% historical events.

Season 1 Conclusion

In the concluding episode of Season 1, titled “The House of Special Purpose,” the focus shifts to the Romanovs’ relocation to Ipatiev House in Yekaterinburg. As the White Army gains the upper hand and converges on Yekaterinburg, Yurovsky assumes control of the house’s security and orchestrates the execution of the family. Eighteen members meet their tragic end, while a few manage to evade the fate. Subsequently, President Boris Yeltsin issues orders to inter the remaining family members. In 2007, two members of the family were discovered, and to date, their remains remain unburied.
